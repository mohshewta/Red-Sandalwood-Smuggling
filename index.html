Introduction

Red sandalwood smuggling has become a major concern in India and across international borders. This rare wood, known for its deep red hue and medicinal properties, is illegally harvested and traded despite strict regulations.

Why does it happen?

High demand from foreign markets, especially in East Asia, drives smuggling. Red sandalwood fetches astronomical prices due to its use in musical instruments, luxury furniture, and traditional medicine.

Impact of Smuggling

Smuggling causes massive deforestation, loss of biodiversity, and threatens the survival of this valuable tree species. Law enforcement faces immense challenges tackling these organized crime networks.
